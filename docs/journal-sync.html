How your journal stays complete

Even when the app is closed most of the time.

Short answer: yes — your local journal becomes a permanent, ever-growing archive, and Bybit's endpoint is only used to top it up with what's new.

The core idea — two stores, opposite behaviour

Rolling window

Bybit closed-PnL

Only retains recent history (~2 years), then trades fall off the back. It's a source you sip from — not your archive.

Permanent

Your trade_journal.json

Append-only. Nothing is ever removed. Once a trade is written locally it stays forever — even after Bybit stops returning it.

So your local file is a superset that only grows. Bybit is just where you fetch the delta — the trades that happened since the newest one you already have.

The key step is the has-data branch: on every normal run it starts from the newest trade you already have and pulls forward. It does not re-pull a fixed window each time — it pulls exactly the gap.

Your questions, precisely

1Capture everything even if the app's closed most of the time?

Yes  Each time you open it, it fetches everything that closed since your last recorded trade and merges it. Your archive grows monotonically; it never shrinks.

2Does local data persist beyond Bybit's recent window?

Yes, permanently  Bybit's endpoint is only consulted for the delta. A trade you fetched 8 months ago stays in trade_journal.json even after it ages out of Bybit's queryable range. Local is the durable copy.

3Real gaps only if I'm away longer than Bybit's retention?

Exactly right  The threshold is Bybit's retention (~2 years) — not the 7-day window or the 30-day backfill. Go away for 3 months and the next open chunks ~13 seven-day windows from your last trade to now and recovers all of it — zero gap.

You'd only lose data if a trade closed and you never opened the app again until that trade had already fallen off Bybit's ~2-year window. For someone who opens this to place trades, that effectively never happens.

4Duplication of already-seen trades?

Can't happen  Every trade is deduped by its closing order ID against a _seen set that's rebuilt from the journal file on startup (so dedup survives restarts, not just within a session).

It even deliberately re-fetches the boundary trade each time (a 1-second overlap so nothing slips through the crack), and the dedup gate silently drops it. R-multiples are also gated to only-new records, so a re-fetched trade can't re-consume your risk ledger.

The R-multiple match itself is exact for app-managed closes: when a TP/SL child fires (or you market-close from the app), the closing order ID is written into the risk ledger, and the reconcile joins on that ID. Re-entering the same symbol at the same price can't attribute the wrong risk. The fuzzy symbol + entry-price match (±0.1%) survives only as a fallback for trades closed outside the app.

One caveat to act on — the cold start

⚠ First run only: backfills 30 days

The first time you run this code the journal is empty, so it backfills only 30 days (the cold branch). Trades older than 30 days from that first run won't be pulled in. After that it's incremental-from-last-close forever, so this only bites once.

If you want your full history captured on that first launch, set this in .env for the first run, then remove it:

JOURNAL_BACKFILL_DAYS=730

That backfills ~2 years (Bybit's retention) in 7-day chunks on first launch — slower (many requests), but a one-time pull. From then on every open just sips the delta.

Footnote: closedPnl is net of trading fees but not funding, so very long holds will read slightly rosy in the journal.
